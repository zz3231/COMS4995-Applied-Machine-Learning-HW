\section{Introduction}

This report presents a \textbf{minimal causal Transformer} built from scratch in PyTorch for \textbf{next-token prediction} on Tiny Shakespeare ($\sim$1.1\,M chars). The training objective is autoregressive language modeling: maximize $\log p(x_t \mid x_{<t})$ via cross-entropy. We report $\mathrm{PPL} = \exp(\mathrm{CE}_{\mathrm{val}})$; lower is better.

The architecture uses two Transformer blocks, $d_{\mathrm{model}}=128$, eight heads, FFN width 512, RMSNorm, sinusoidal PE, and causal self-attention. Tokenization is BPE with vocab $\leq 500$, fit on training text only. Beyond the main run we present a \textbf{PE ablation}, a \textbf{shorter-context} study, and qualitative generation.

%%% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Methods}

\subsection{Data and tokenization}
The first 80\% of characters form \texttt{train\_text}; the rest is \texttt{val\_text}. A ByteLevel BPE tokenizer is trained on \texttt{train\_text} alone (\texttt{vocab\_size}$=$500, \texttt{min\_frequency}$=$2). Overlapping windows of \textbf{65} tokens with stride \textbf{32} yield $\sim$12.8k training sequences, each providing 64 supervised next-token positions.

\subsection{Architecture}
Pre-LN blocks: $x \leftarrow x + \mathrm{Drop}(\mathrm{Sublayer}(\mathrm{RMSNorm}(x)))$ for both attention and FFN. Causal MHA computes $\mathrm{softmax}(QK^\top\!/\sqrt{d_k} + M)\,V$ where $M$ is $-\infty$ above the diagonal. Sinusoidal PE is added to embeddings scaled by $\sqrt{d_{\mathrm{model}}}$. A final RMSNorm + linear head produces vocabulary logits ($\sim$$10^6$ params total).

\subsection{Training}
AdamW (lr $3\!\times\!10^{-4}$, wd $0.01$), batch 32, dropout 0.1, gradient clip 1.0, 25 epochs, fixed seed. Validation uses \texttt{torch.no\_grad()}.

\subsection{Experiments}
\textbf{(A) PE ablation:} same architecture without sinusoidal PE, 15 epochs. Figure~\ref{fig:ablate} aligns the first 15 epochs of the main model against the full no-PE run for epoch-matched comparison.
\textbf{(B) Shorter context:} block 33 / stride 16 (32 positions), 12 epochs. Not directly comparable to the main model (different $T$, windows, epochs, initialization); probes the $O(T^2)$ compute tradeoff.

\section{Results}

\subsection{Training curves and perplexity}

Figure~\ref{fig:loss} shows CE and PPL over 25 epochs. Train CE drops steeply (5.06 $\to$ 3.74 by epoch 5) then decelerates to \textbf{3.38}; val CE plateaus earlier at \textbf{3.72} (PPL $=$ \textbf{41.33}). The widening train--val gap after epoch $\sim$5 signals mild overfitting: the model memorizes training n-grams faster than it generalizes, though the gap stays modest (0.34 nats by epoch 25). PPL improves most dramatically in the first five epochs (83.5 $\to$ 48.6) with diminishing returns thereafter.

\subsection{Attention visualization and analysis}
\label{sec:attention}

Figures~\ref{fig:att1}--\ref{fig:att2} show causal attention for a validation prefix that reads approximately \emph{``you are, That is, a woman; if you be more, you're none; If you be''} (each axis cell is one BPE token). Both maps are from the \textbf{converged} model (epoch 25); the strictly \textbf{lower-triangular} structure confirms the causal mask.

\paragraph{Layer 0, head 0 (Figure~\ref{fig:att1}).}
\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Diagonal / recency bias.} Early tokens (``y'', ``ou'', ``are'') place most weight on themselves or their immediate predecessor, forming a bright band along the diagonal---typical of lower-layer heads that propagate local subword identity into the residual stream.
  \item \textbf{Content-anchored links.} ``om'' (from ``woman'') attends sharply to ``are'' (weight $\approx$0.9), suggesting the model has learned a distributional association between the copula and its noun complement. The semicolon at position 12 focuses on the preceding ``if'', linking the conditional clause head.
  \item \textbf{Diffuse spread for later queries.} Positions with $\geq$15 available keys show broad, light shading across many past tokens rather than sharp peaks---this head mixes wide context for downstream layers.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Layer 1, head 2 (Figure~\ref{fig:att2}).}
Compared to L0H0, this deeper head shows \textbf{sparser, more peaked} attention:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Long-range ``you''-tracking.} ``is'' (pos.\ 6) attends strongly back to ``ou'' (pos.\ 2, part of ``you'')---a four-token span. ``more'' (pos.\ 16) concentrates weight $\approx$0.7 on ``you'' (pos.\ 14); ``re'' (pos.\ 20, part of ``you're'') reaches back to ``you'' as well. This head appears to \textbf{track the pronoun ``you''} across the sequence, a rudimentary subject-tracking role.
  \item \textbf{Punctuation as clause anchors.} Commas and semicolons receive concentrated attention from later queries, serving as positional landmarks for clause boundaries.
  \item \textbf{Head diversity.} Comparing the same prefix across two heads reveals clear functional differentiation: L0H0 favors local, diffuse mixing; L1H2 favors sparse, long-range content-specific links. This division of labor mirrors findings in larger Transformer LMs and shows that multi-head attention learns complementary features even at small scale.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Limitation.} Heatmaps are from the converged model only; we did not render attention at early epochs. However, the steep initial PPL drop suggests attention specialization likely emerges within the first few epochs.

\subsection{Positional encoding ablation}

At epoch 15 the main model reaches PPL \textbf{43.13} while the no-PE model reaches \textbf{43.58}---a gap of 0.45 (Figure~\ref{fig:ablate}). The PE model is lower at \textbf{nearly every epoch}, confirming a systematic benefit. The gap is small because (i) the corpus has strong local statistical cues (capitalized line-starts, newline patterns) that partially encode position even without PE, and (ii) with only $T=64$, positional ambiguity is limited. Over the full 25-epoch main run PPL drops further to 41.33; since the no-PE run was capped at 15 epochs, we cannot attribute the entire 2.25-point difference to PE.

\subsection{Shorter-context experiment}

With block 33 / stride 16 the model starts at PPL $\sim$63 and reaches \textbf{40.19} by epoch 12 (Figure~\ref{fig:short}). Starting PPL is lower than the main model's $\sim$83 because shorter windows average over fewer conditioning tokens, making the per-position prediction ``easier.'' The key takeaway is \textbf{operational}: halving $T$ (64 $\to$ 32) reduces the attention tensor from $B\!\times\!H\!\times\!64\!\times\!64$ to $B\!\times\!H\!\times\!32\!\times\!32$---a $4\times$ savings in attention memory and FLOPs---at the cost of losing long-range dependencies beyond 32 tokens.

\subsection{Qualitative generation}

Temperature-0.85 sampling from the trained model produces Shakespeare-\emph{flavored} but incoherent text: \emph{``you are, That is, a woman; if you be more, you'er's thereock, For thought, prifft being in the dieves\ldots''}. The model captures comma-separated clause structure, pseudo-archaic pronouns (``Ay'', ``Ty''), and stage-direction labels, but hallucinates many subwords (``thereock'', ``prifft''). This is expected given $\sim$$10^6$ params, 500-token vocab, and 25 epochs of training.

\section{Discussion}

\textbf{Attention patterns.}
The two visualized heads (\S\ref{sec:attention}) show complementary roles: L0H0 acts as a \emph{local mixer} (diagonal recency, diffuse spread), while L1H2 is a \emph{content pointer} (sparse ``you''-tracking, punctuation anchoring). This early-layer-local / later-layer-global stratification mirrors findings in large-scale LMs and demonstrates that functional head specialization emerges even in a two-layer model.

\textbf{Hyperparameter stability.}
Learning rate was the most sensitive knob: values above $\sim$$10^{-3}$ caused loss spikes, below $\sim$$10^{-4}$ slowed convergence. Gradient clipping (norm 1.0) prevented the occasional explosion that AdamW alone could not absorb. Batch size 32 and weight decay 0.01 were stable defaults.

\textbf{Positional encodings.}
Self-attention without PE is permutation-invariant: it treats any token permutation identically. Sinusoidal PE breaks this symmetry; our ablation confirms a consistent but small benefit. RoPE (rotary PE) is an alternative that injects position into $Q,K$ products rather than additive embeddings, potentially offering better length extrapolation.

\textbf{Runtime bottlenecks.}
The dominant cost is the $O(BHT^2)$ attention score tensor. The short-context experiment directly demonstrates the $4\times$ savings from halving $T$. Modern solutions include FlashAttention (fused softmax kernel, avoids materializing $T\!\times\!T$) and KV caching at inference (avoids recomputing past keys/values during autoregressive generation); neither was implemented here.

\textbf{Course context.}
This assignment performs \textbf{supervised} next-token prediction with a fixed CE loss. This contrasts with RLHF/PPO (L11), where a policy gradient optimizes a learned reward model trained on human preferences---our work sits on the ``pretraining'' side of that divide.
